## Commitment

Accessibility isn't a feature or a checklist — it's a baseline. This site is designed to meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA as a minimum. That means sufficient colour contrast, keyboard navigability, screen reader compatibility, and content that works regardless of how it's being accessed.

Given that a significant portion of this portfolio is writing about accessibility practice, it would be a meaningful failure if the site itself didn't hold up to scrutiny.

## What's been done

Colour contrast has been audited across light and dark modes — all text meets or exceeds the 4.5:1 AA ratio against its background. The site uses semantic HTML throughout: landmarks, headings, and lists are used for structure rather than presentation. All interactive elements are keyboard accessible and have visible focus styles. Decorative SVG icons are hidden from assistive technology; meaningful content is not. The site is a member of the [a11y-webring.club](https://a11y-webring.club/), a webring for accessibility-focused personal sites.

## What's been tested

The site has been tested with VoiceOver on macOS and Safari, and reviewed with keyboard-only navigation. Automated checks have been run using axe. Testing on Windows with NVDA and JAWS is on the to-do list — if you use either and encounter issues, please get in touch.

## Known limitations

Artifact placeholders on case study pages are visual representations of design work — screen reader users will encounter a text label describing what the artifact is, but not the artifact itself. Where case studies reference specific visual decisions, those decisions are also described in the surrounding prose.

The vertical year stamp in the bottom-right corner of each page is readable text but carries no navigational or structural significance — it's present for sighted users and available to screen reader users in the page footer.

## Get in touch

If you encounter anything on this site that doesn't work for you — whether it's a contrast issue, a navigation problem, missing alternative text, or anything else — please email [your@email.com](mailto:your@email.com). I'll aim to respond within a week and fix genuine issues promptly.

Feedback from people who actually use assistive technology is more useful than any automated audit. It's always welcome.
